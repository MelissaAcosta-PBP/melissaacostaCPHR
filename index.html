Analytics & Workforce Reporting

```
★ Featured · People Analytics

Multi-Year Compensation Model

The organization lacked longitudinal compensation visibility. Salary decisions were reactive, disconnected from trends, and difficult to defend to the board without a structured evidence base.

Outcome

Built a multi-year model capturing beginning-of-year and end-of-year salaries, salary movement, percentage increases, tenure, job level, department, province, and employee group, creating the first structured foundation for equity conversations and board-level compensation reporting.

People Analytics

Compensation Benchmarking & Salary Trend Analysis

Leadership needed to understand how salaries compared internally and against the market, especially important for retention, fairness, and compensation governance in a competitive labour market.

Outcome

Conducted benchmarking using internal salary history and external market data, helping leadership understand pay positioning, market movement, and areas requiring further review.

People Analytics

Compa-Ratio Analysis

Salary amounts alone don't show whether employees are positioned appropriately within a band. A more rigorous view of pay positioning was needed to support equity-focused decisions.

Outcome

Applied compa-ratio analysis to assess pay positioning, identify internal equity considerations, and provide structured input to compensation planning conversations.

Executive Reporting

Board-Ready Compensation & Workforce Reporting

Senior leaders and board members needed concise, credible people data that could support governance-level conversations about compensation strategy and workforce risk.

Outcome

Prepared analysis and reports that translated complex people data into clear narratives for board audiences, strengthening the organization's ability to discuss workforce trends with confidence.

Workforce Reporting

Annual Headcount Tracking Report

Without consistent tracking, it was harder to support planning, budgeting, and people strategy in a distributed organization spread across HQ and project-based teams.

Outcome

Created headcount tracking across department, province, employment group, and organizational segment, giving leadership a reliable view of workforce structure for planning conversations.

Workforce Reporting

Total Cost of Employment & Payroll Summary

Leaders needed visibility into the full cost of employment beyond base salary — including statutory costs like CPP and EI — to support budgeting and funding decisions.

Outcome

Created a statutory payroll cost summary comparing gross pay, employer costs, funded amounts, and cost variances, improving financial visibility for People, Finance, and leadership.

Workforce Reporting

Payroll Allocation & Cost Distribution Model

Employees split across multiple projects required accurate, consistent allocation of payroll costs with reliable pay-cycle record preservation.

Outcome

Helped design a Google Sheets-based model supporting live data, pay-cycle snapshots, project splits, and reconciliation logic, strengthening payroll distribution and reporting accuracy.

HR Operations

People & Culture Metrics & Cadence Framework

People data existed across many areas but there was no operating rhythm for what should be reviewed, when, and by whom. A cadence framework was needed to make P&C work more strategic.

Outcome

Developed a metrics and cadence plan across payroll, compensation, talent acquisition, onboarding, retention, performance, engagement, learning, DEIB, wellbeing, and annual policy cycles.
```

02

Employee Listening & Engagement

```
★ Featured · Employee Listening

Multi-Wave Engagement Survey Strategy & Dashboard

8 to 10 survey waves over four years with shifting question sets made longitudinal analysis nearly impossible. The organization needed a way to track employee sentiment over time without losing comparability.

Outcome

Developed a construct-level aggregation approach to normalize engagement data across waves, mapping shifting questions to 6 to 8 stable meta-themes, making longitudinal trend analysis possible for the first time. Built accompanying dashboards showing favorability, eNPS, HQ vs. Projects comparisons, and dimension-level results.

Employee Listening

Employee Listening Strategy

The organization needed a more structured approach to understanding employee experience beyond anecdotal feedback or one-off conversations in a remote, distributed environment.

Outcome

Built a mature listening approach using biannual surveys, pulse checks, qualitative channels, and eNPS tracking, helping leadership identify patterns and prioritize action based on evidence.

Analytics

Engagement Analytics Dashboard

Raw survey results alone didn't give enough context to understand trends, team differences, or priority areas. The data needed to be translated into usable leadership insights.

Outcome

Created reporting showing favorability scores, eNPS movement, dimension-level results, and key gaps, helping leaders move from "what did employees say?" to "what do we do next?"

Manager Enablement

Manager Engagement Reporting Pack

Broad organizational summaries don't translate into better day-to-day leadership. Managers needed tailored, practical insights to understand where their teams needed support.

Outcome

Developed manager-facing reporting with KPI tables, segment comparisons, gap analysis, and prioritized recommendations to support manager accountability and targeted action planning.

IFX Networks

Regional Engagement & NPS Measurement

Understanding employee sentiment across multiple countries during growth and transformation was essential for morale, communication, and leadership trust.

Outcome

Led regional measurement using NPS-style surveys, qualitative feedback, and leadership reporting, surfacing employee concerns and informing action plans across the region.

PTO / Vacation Utilization Dashboard

Built a dashboard using normalized HRIS data to show employee-level and organization-level vacation usage, tracking progress against a June OKR and supporting manager conversations about workload and wellbeing.

Tied to Organizational OKR

Employee Vacation Risk Analysis

Created an employee-level risk view with entitlement, vacation used, percentage used, burnout risk flag, zero-vacation flag, and risk priority, moving the dashboard from passive reporting to practical manager action.
```

04

Talent Systems & Onboarding

```
★ Featured · Service Design

Onboarding Redesign: Service Blueprint & Journey Mapping

Onboarding depended too heavily on individual managers and fragmented resources. New hires in a remote organization needed a more consistent, structured experience, and the organization needed to understand what was happening from the employee's perspective.

Outcome

Led a 6-month redesign using service design methods including journey mapping and a full service blueprint. Mapped frontstage employee touchpoints, backstage HR activities, systems, handoffs, and improvement opportunities, then co-created department-specific onboarding standards with managers across the organization.

Onboarding

Notion Onboarding Knowledge Hub

Information was scattered across systems and informal practices. New employees needed a centralized, self-service entry point that worked in a fully remote environment.

Outcome

Built a Notion hub with structured guidance for Day 1, Week 1, Weeks 2 to 3, and Month 1, improving consistency, accessibility, and self-service access for new hires.

IFX Networks

IFX DNA Challenge: Regional Onboarding Program

A multi-country technology company needed a consistent onboarding experience that connected employees to culture, values, products, and ways of working across 9 countries.

Outcome

Designed and launched standardized onboarding across 9 countries, strengthening cultural integration, organizational knowledge, and new hire connection at scale.

Performance

Growth Goal Tracking System

The organization had an OKR requiring individual growth goals but no clear way to track progress, manager approvals, or accountability across teams.

Outcome

Built a Notion-based tracking system with statuses, manager approvals, and completion visibility, improving follow-through and supporting the organization's OKR accountability.

Performance

Performance Cycle Adoption & Manager Accountability

Performance processes only create value when managers and employees consistently participate. The organization needed better visibility into adoption and follow-through.

Outcome

Created tracking and reporting mechanisms to monitor participation, identify gaps, and support manager accountability through performance and growth cycles.

IFX Networks

Performance Management Platform Implementation

IFX had manual, Excel-based performance processes that were difficult to scale across 9 countries. A structured platform was needed for goals, competencies, calibration, and visibility.

Outcome

Led implementation of a regional performance management platform, replacing manual tracking and improving structure, consistency, and leadership visibility across performance cycles.
```
